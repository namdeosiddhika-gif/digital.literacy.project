Here is a checklist of essential Do’s and Don’ts for responsible social media use:

Do’s
Audit your privacy settings regularly to ensure you know exactly who can see your personal posts and tagged photos.
Think before you tag. Always ask for permission before posting photos of friends or classmates in social or academic settings. 
Network professionally by creating a LinkedIn profile that highlights your projects, skills, and academic achievements.
Fact-check before sharing. Verify the credibility of news or academic information before reposting it to avoid spreading misinformation.
Use your platform for good. Share your achievements, volunteer work, or insights into your field of study to build a positive personal brand.

Don’ts
Don't post impulsive rants or emotionally charged content about professors, classes, or peers that you might regret later.
Don't share sensitive information, such as your specific dorm room number, current real-time location, or student ID details.
Don't engage with "trolls" or cyberbullies. Block or report accounts that exhibit toxic behavior rather than escalating the conflict.
Don't post content depicting illegal acts or violations of your university’s code of conduct, even if the post is intended as a "joke."
Don't overshare. Avoid posting every detail of your daily life; maintain a healthy boundary between your private life and your public persona.
